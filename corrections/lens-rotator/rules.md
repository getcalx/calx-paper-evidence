## Seed Rules

1. **Lead with the blind spot.** The most valuable output is always the thing the author didn't see. Put it first, not buried at the end.
2. **Cross-reference over single-lens.** Intersections between two frameworks are more valuable than any single lens applied alone.
3. **Match depth to stakes.** Tactical decisions get 3 lenses and a paragraph. Strategic bets get a full rotation.
4. **Specificity or silence.** If you can't ground an observation in a specific framework with a specific recommendation, don't include it.
5. **Reviewer-SY1 and Reviewer-BH2 are almost always relevant.** Fragility analysis and choice architecture apply to nearly every product and business decision. Start there when unsure.
6. **Don't rotate for show.** If only 4 lenses reveal something useful, stop at 4. Adding 6 more shallow observations dilutes the signal.
7. **The synthesis is the deliverable.** People skim the individual lenses. They read the synthesis. Make it sharp.
8. **ACE = Agentic Context Engineering** ("Evolving Contexts for Self-Improving Language Models"). Spencer built the reinforcement loop first, paper validated it after. Never frame as adopted — frame as productized. This is a founder execution signal.
9. **Track position evolution across evaluations.** When running Prism on related topics, reference prior evals. If a thinker changes position, flag it explicitly — position changes are high-value signal.
10. **Flag independent convergence from orthogonal frameworks.** When thinkers from different clusters (e.g., behavioral + systems) land on the same conclusion independently, that finding outranks any single-framework recommendation.
11. **Splits can be findings.** Don't always force a resolution on contradictions. A 4/4 split with upstream consensus is more useful than a forced 6/4 resolution. Present the split, identify what's upstream, let Spencer decide.
12. **Compound decision types produce richer output.** Strategy + category + product + fundraising evaluations give thinkers more surface area. Worth the extra tokens for high-stakes decisions.
13. **Write full thinker reports to a file.** Sub-agent outputs disappear after synthesis. Always persist the full unedited reports as a companion document so Spencer can read the raw arguments.
14. **Include Reviewer-GM2 when evaluating GTM, distribution, or marketing strategy.** Framework-heavy thinkers analyze internal logic. Practitioner lenses catch execution blind spots — especially "who is the distribution mechanism?" The original 10-thinker strategy eval missed "founder is invisible" entirely. Don't leave the practitioner out of GTM evals.
15. **Use practitioners to resolve framework tensions.** When two framework thinkers disagree (e.g., Reviewer-GM3 vs. Reviewer-ST2 on category timing), check if a practitioner lens reveals the disagreement is about sequencing/execution, not strategy. Reviewer-GM2 resolved the category timing tension by distinguishing "publishing about the phenomenon" from "premature category declaration."
16. **Prism has a content review mode.** Strategy evals assess *what to do*. Content reviews assess *how well you did it*. For content drafts, route through practitioner lenses (Reviewer-GM2) for craft-level feedback — rhythm, voice, fold point, post-publish tactics — not just strategic alignment.
