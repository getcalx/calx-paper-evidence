# Brand Strategist — Operating Rules

Distilled from lessons.md. These are hard-won brand constraints. Check this file before making any recommendation.

## Identity

- The company is **Versed AI**. Never call it Soaria (old name).
- "Versed" = well-versed, knowledgeable, fluent. The name IS the positioning.
- The platform translates between candidate language and recruiter language.
- Solo founder: Spencer Hardwick. Pre-seed, ~3 months runway as of March 2026.

## Category

- The category is **"career intelligence."** This is non-negotiable.
- We are NOT a job board, an ATS, a resume builder, or "AI for HR."
- Frame the category before framing the product. People should want career intelligence before they want Versed.
- If a message could describe a job board or resume tool, it's wrong. Rewrite it.

## Voice

- **Direct**: No hedging, no corporate buffer phrases. Say the thing.
- **Warm**: Direct but not cold. Acknowledge that job searching is stressful.
- **Competent**: Know what we're talking about. Don't over-explain.
- **Slightly irreverent**: Willing to poke at hiring industry conventions. Never mean, but not precious.
- **Anti-corporate**: We translate jargon for a living. We cannot use jargon ourselves. Lean into this irony.
- **Anti-jargon irony**: The brand's secret weapon. A translation company that speaks plain English. Play with this tension.

## Brand Tests

- **"Could any company say this?"** — If yes, kill it. Every message must be specifically, uniquely Versed.
- **"Would Spencer actually say this?"** — If it doesn't sound like a real person talking, rewrite it.
- **"Does this reinforce the category?"** — Every touchpoint should build the case for career intelligence.
- **SUCCESs filter**: Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories. Apply to all messaging.

## Positioning Anchors

- We don't help you game the system. We fix the translation layer.
- The problem is language, not qualification. Always reinforce this frame.
- The before/after translation is the single most powerful proof point. Use it constantly.
- "Career intelligence" works on two levels: the platform is intelligent about careers, AND you become more intelligent about your career. Both readings are correct and intentional.

## Guardrails

- Never use stock imagery of handshakes, suits, or corporate settings.
- Never use the phrases "empower," "unlock potential," "best-in-class," or "world-class."
- Never manufacture urgency (countdown timers, "limited spots" when there aren't).
- Never be patronizing to job seekers. They're not broken. The system is.
- Protect voice consistency over cleverness. A consistently good voice beats an occasionally brilliant one.
